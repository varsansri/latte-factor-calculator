Latte Factor Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

What is the latte factor?

The latte factor is a personal finance concept showing how small daily habits (like a $5 coffee) compound into massive lost wealth. Invested at 7% over 20 years, that daily $5 becomes ~$96,000. The question isn't whether to drink coffee — it's whether you know the real cost.

How much does a daily $5 coffee cost over 20 years?

Direct spending: $1,825/year × 20 = $36,500. Opportunity cost (invested at 7%): approximately $96,000. The wealth gap is the money that compounds silently while you're buying coffee. Enter your numbers above to see your exact figure.

What other habits can I calculate with this tool?

Any recurring expense: daily takeout ($12–20), cigarettes ($7–15/day), energy drinks ($3–5), unused subscriptions ($10–50/month), daily alcohol, or impulse buys. The tool works for any habit — just enter the cost and frequency.

Is the latte factor concept accurate?

The compound interest math is 100% accurate. Critics argue income and housing costs matter more to wealth-building — and they're right. But the latte factor is useful for noticing unconscious daily spending and deciding which habits align with your wealth goals.

What investment return should I use in the calculator?

The S&P 500 has historically averaged ~10% annually before inflation. A conservative realistic estimate is 7% (accounting for inflation and fees). Use 7% for a realistic baseline, 10% if you're optimistic, and 4–6% if you invest in bonds or balanced funds.

The True Cost of Daily Habits — Latte Factor Wealth Calculator

The latte factor concept reveals a truth most people never calculate: small, automatic daily expenses don't just cost what you pay — they cost the compounded wealth you never build. A $5 daily coffee is $1,825 per year. Invested consistently at 7% annual return, that grows to over $96,000 in 20 years. The coffee didn't just cost $5. It cost you $96,000.

This calculator uses the same compound interest formula used by financial planners: FV = PMT × [((1 + r)^n − 1) / r] × (1 + r). It converts your daily habit cost into monthly contributions and projects the future value if you had invested that money instead. The result isn't meant to make you feel guilty — it's meant to make your financial choices conscious rather than automatic.

Apply this to any habit: cigarettes, takeout lunches, streaming subscriptions you barely use, or daily energy drinks. The equivalents section puts the number in perspective — you may find that one habit is costing you a year's rent, a car, or multiple international trips per decade. Use the insight to decide what's actually worth it to you.
